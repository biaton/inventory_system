2. Procurement (Purchase Orders)

The Procurement module supports Multi-Supplier Batching. This means you can create a single Main P.O. containing multiple orders from different suppliers simultaneously, and approve them as one entire batch.

A. Purchase Order Entry (Drafting)

1. Global P.O. Settings

Set the Main PO No. (click GEN to auto-generate), Global Delivery Date, and Transport. These settings will apply to the entire batch.

2. Dynamic Supplier Blocks

Add a supplier block for each vendor. The Contact Person will automatically load upon selecting a supplier. Set the Tax Term (e.g., VAT Inclusive) and Discount (%).

3. Item Encoding & Auto-Compute

Upon typing the Item Code, the system will automatically fill in the Description and Unit Price from the Masterlist. The system will handle the computation for Subtotal, Tax, and Grand Total.

B. Supplier PO Draft Review

Before saving to the database, it will pass through a Confirmation Page. Here, you can review the comprehensive summary of all suppliers in the batch. Click Confirm & Push to send it to the approval queue.

C. Authorization Queue (Approval)

Managers/admins will use the P.O. Approval Module.

  • Click the icon to review the entire batch content before making a decision.
  • Upon clicking Approve, the system will send an Email Notification back to the user who created the PO to inform them it has been approved.

D. P.O. Inquiry & Printing

Use the Advanced Search (Date range, PO No., Supplier) to find previous orders. If the status is Approved, you can click the icon to print the Official Purchase Order Document.

E. P.O. Correction Module

If there are errors in price or quantity after creating the PO, use the Correction Module.

  • Find the order using the Main PO No. or Batch Reference.
  • A Reason for Correction must be provided as part of the security audit trail.
  • Edit the New Qty and New Price in the dynamic grid.
  • System Rule: Saving corrections will automatically revert the PO status back to PENDING APPROVAL and recalculate all Taxes and Discounts.

3. Inbound Logistics

The Inbound Logistics module manages incoming deliveries from suppliers. This includes importing delivery schedules, visual calendar tracking, and physical dock arrival verification before items are moved into the warehouse.

A. Mass Schedule Import

For bulk deliveries, use the Import Delivery tool. You can download the system template and upload it back as a .CSV or .XLSX file to instantly register the entire delivery schedule into the system.

B. Logistics Scheduler

A visual map of all expected deliveries (Calendar View). On-time deliveries are colored Blue, while late shipments are highlighted in Red. Clicking an event will open the shipment manifest.

C. Advance Shipping Notice (ASN)

Once the supplier has handed the items to the courier, use this module to confirm the stock is in transit. Enter the Freight / Courier Partner, Master Tracking No., and Updated ETA. This will automatically change the P.O. status to In Transit.

D. Gate / Dock Arrival Verification

When the truck physically arrives at the warehouse gate, the guard or logistics receiver must verify the following before allowing entry:

  • Arrival Time & Remarks: Log the arrival time and vehicle details.
  • Actual Pcs & Box Count: Verify and encode the number of arriving boxes/cartons (Handling Units).
  • Visual Condition: Record if the box is secure (Good / Sealed) or damaged (Damaged Box, Wet Carton, Open Seal).
Next Step: Upon clicking Approve & Send to Receiving, the shipment will move to the Receiving Module to be opened, undergo QC (Quality Control), and receive a Material Tag barcode.

4. Receiving & Storage Operations

This module covers the entire lifecycle of inventory from its arrival at the warehouse (Receiving and Material Tagging) to its dispatch to another department or customer (Order Picking and Delivery Requests).

Phase 1: PO Receiving & QC

After the items arrive, they must be confirmed in the system using the Receive via PO module.

  1. Scan or type the Main PO or Sub-PO Barcode and click Load.
  2. Review the list. You can input how many items were actually received.
  3. Set the QC Inspection Status (Pending, Passed, or Quarantine).
  4. Click Confirm Receipt & Generate Tags. This will automatically update the PO status and redirect you to the Material Tag Generator.
Cross-Dock Interception: If the scanned PO has a waiting Customer Order, the system will show a flashing alert. This item will be routed directly to the Dispatch Area and will not be stored in a rack.

Phase 2: Material Tag Generator & Printing

Every box entering the warehouse needs its own "Birth Certificate" or Material Tag (Lot Number).

Auto-Generation

Click the Auto-Generate button to create a random and unique Lot Number (e.g., L-2605-1234).

Container & Packing Calculation

If 100 PCS are in 5 boxes, set the Container Count = 5. The system will print 5 separate tags to be attached to each box.

Label Print View

Clicking "Generate & Print Tags" will produce a print-ready layout (100x150mm format) complete with a high-quality CODE39 Barcode and physical inspection grid. Simply click the floating "Start Printing" button in the upper-right of the screen.

Phase 3: Storage & Put-Away

After being tagged, items must be mapped to a physical warehouse rack.

  1. Scan the Location Barcode (e.g., RACK-A1) on the wall or shelf.
  2. Scan the Material Tag / Lot No. of the box.
  3. Click Register Storage to lock the item in that location.

Phase 4: Delivery Request & Movement Slip

This is used to create a Movement Slip when inventory needs to be transferred from the warehouse to production or a client. You can use the Search Material modal or Bulk Excel Import for rapid data entry.

Interactive Print Setup (Movement Slip)

Before finalizing the document for print, the system provides a live Print Settings Sidebar that won't appear on the printed page. Here you can customize the form's appearance:

1. Theme Color & QR Sizing

You can change the color of borders/headers using the color picker. You can also enlarge or hide the Header QR code.

2. Visibility Toggles

You can toggle the visibility of Item Barcodes (CODE128) and the Signatures Area to save space.

3. Custom Instructions

You can type a warning (e.g., "URGENT TRANSFER") that will appear at the top of the form prior to printing, ensuring immediate attention from the guard or receiver.

Phase 5: Order Picking (FIFO Enforcement)

This is where actual warehouse stock is deducted based on the created Delivery Request.

  1. Scan the Request No. to load the Picking List.
  2. The system will indicate if there is sufficient stock or a shortage (Shortage!).
  3. Click Auto Allocation to automatically assign the maximum permissible amount from the rack, strictly enforcing the FIFO (First-In, First-Out) rule.
  4. Click Register to deduct inventory and fulfill the request.

5. Outbound (Customer Orders)

This module drives the complete fulfillment cycle—from receiving the client order and retrieving warehouse stock (Smart Picking), through to the actual delivery (Logistics Dispatch).

Phase 1: Order Creation (Manual & Excel)

You can create a Customer Order in two ways:

Manual Input

Type each order individually. This supports creating a "Global Batch" where multiple customer orders are scheduled for delivery on the same day.

Bulk Excel Upload

Upload the Sales Order .csv/.xlsx template from Accounting. The system will map Item Codes, Qty, and Prices automatically before saving to the database.

Phase 2: P.O. Correction Module

Before dispatch, a batch can be edited if there are changes in price or quantity. Search for the batch using the Correction Module and enter a Reason for Correction.

* Warning: This resets the orders' status back to PENDING to protect the audit trail.

Phase 3: Smart Order Picking (FIFO)

After confirming the order, it's sent to the warehouse floor to retrieve items. Staff will use the Smart Order Picking screen.

  • AI Suggestion: The system identifies the exact rack/bin to pull items from, enforcing FIFO (First-In, First-Out).
  • Auto-Fill Qty: Click once to populate the maximum required quantity.
  • Confirming the pick automatically deducts physical stock and upgrades the order status to Processing.

Phase 4: Logistics Dispatch

After orders are packed, the Dispatcher goes to Order Inquiry and clicks the Dispatch button to release the cargo.

Load Verification: Scan the Main PO No. with the Poka-Yoke scanner to unlock the Dispatch Button. This prevents releasing the wrong order.

Fleet Assign: Choose from External Couriers (LBC, J&T) or the company's In-House Fleet. (Note: Vehicles subject to Number Coding on that day are hidden).

Phase 5: Print Dispatch Manifest

The system will print a document for the driver and guard detailing the items for transit.

Blind Manifest Mode

Toggle this in Print Settings to hide prices (Unit Price and Amount) on the printed copy as a security measure.

Page Breaking / Scissor Tool

Click the Scissor icon next to the table to break the printout if orders are being sent in different trucks.

Phase 6: Electronic Proof of Delivery (e-POD)

When the driver returns, click Mark Delivered. Enter the receiver's name and date. The system sends a confirmation email to the client. If the in-house fleet was used, it automatically requests Fuel Cost and Toll Fees to record expenses in Logistics Analytics.

6. Inventory Request (Internal)

This module handles all internal material requisitions, such as raw materials for Assembly Lines, maintenance tools, or packaging supplies.

A. Creating a New Request

Go to Inventory Request > New Request. Fill in the Department, Required Date, and Purpose. Add item rows by searching the Item Code, input the quantity needed, and click Submit Request.

B. Warehouse Fulfillment (Picking)

Warehouse staff will fulfill the request using the Fulfillment Scanner Workspace.

  1. Go to My Requests and click the action button to open the Workspace.
  2. The system displays a Required Items Checklist.
  3. Scan the physical box/tag (Source Tag ID) and input the quantity being issued.
  4. Click Issue Part Now to automatically deduct the stock using FIFO logic.

C. Material Return Slip (RTS)

If a department has excess materials or was issued the wrong item, use the Return Slip module. Input the Original Request No. (e.g., REQ-2026...), select the item and the exact Lot Number, and submit. This adds the physical stock back into the system inventory.

7. Inventory Processing & Control

This module is the core control center for physical warehouse movements, stock corrections, and advanced routing like Cross-Docking.

Location Transfer

Used for moving stocks from one rack/bin to another.

  1. Scan the Target Lot Number (Material Tag).
  2. The system locks the item and displays its current location.
  3. Scan or type the New Destination Rack.
  4. Click Confirm Transfer.

Stock Out Terminal

Used for manual or ad-hoc deduction of inventory.

  1. Scan the Lot Number of the item to be issued.
  2. Enter the Quantity to Deduct.
  3. Add a Remark/Reference (e.g. For sample, testing).
  4. Confirm Stock Out.

Master Correction (Override)

Used to fix data entry errors or physical count discrepancies. Warning: This permanently overwrites master database records.

Scan the Lot Number to load the system data. You can then forcefully change the exact Quantity and Location. A valid Justification/Reason must be selected to log the audit trail.

Cross-Docking Planner

Matches an incoming Supplier PO directly to a waiting Customer Order (SO). This tells the Receiving Guard to bypass the storage racks and bring the items straight to the Dispatch Area.

  • Select an Incoming Stock (PO) from the left table.
  • Select a Waiting Order (SO) from the right table.
  • Input the Cross-Dock Quantity to allocate.
  • Click Create Link. The guard will see a red blinker when they scan this PO.

8. Inquiry & Reports

A comprehensive suite for tracking stock levels, monitoring system health, and viewing detailed audit trails.

A. Inventory Overview (Global Summary)

Located at Inventory Inquiry > Item Lookup. This groups identical items together, showing the combined stock across all lots.

AI Forecasting (Days of Supply): The system calculates the Average Daily Consumption (ADC) based on the last 30 days of stock outs. It uses this to predict how many days your current stock will last.
  • GREEN = Healthy (> 15 Days)
  • YELLOW = Low Stock Alert
  • RED = Out of Stock or Critical

B. Stock Masterlist & Inline Editing

Displays every single box or lot inside the warehouse. You can search by Rack, Lot No, or Company.

Quick Edit Feature

Directly click on the cells for Lot No, Revision, Invoice No, Expiry Date, and Unit Price inside the table to update them instantly without leaving the page.

Stock Movement Ledger (Bin Card)

Clicking "Stock I/O" on any item opens its individual Bin Card. It records exactly who moved the item, when, and how many were deducted or added.

Global Audit Trail

Located at System Modules > Audit Trail. Shows a master view of every transaction (REG, OUT, MOVE, ADJ) across the entire warehouse.

9. Asset & WIP Assembly

The Assembly module is used for Work-In-Progress (WIP) tracking. It allows production staff to construct machines or equipment by consuming raw materials (tags) from the warehouse.

A. Registering a New Asset

From the Assembly Dashboard, click the New button. Enter the Asset Code (e.g., MAC-001) and the Model Name. The new machine will appear in the registry with a Building status.

B. The WIP Workspace (Part Execution)

Clicking the folder icon opens the Machine Workspace. Here you can execute actions to build or modify the asset:

Action: Assemble (IN)

Scan a material tag and input the quantity. The system will deduct this from the warehouse and lock it into the machine's BOM (Bill of Materials).

Action: Dismantle (OUT)

Removes a part from the machine and returns it to physical inventory. This sets the machine status to Partial.

Every time a part is assembled, the system generates a Thermal Barcode Label for physical tagging of the machine component.

C. Finalizing the Build

Once all parts are installed, click the Finish Build button at the top right of the workspace. This locks the asset, changes its status to Available, and triggers an email alert to management.

10. User Access Control

Administrators use this module to manage employee accounts and assign Role-Based Access Control (RBAC).

Registering a New Employee

Click Register New User. Enter their Name, valid Email Address, and assign a System Role (e.g., Warehouse Staff, Purchasing).

Security Protocol: You do not set their password manually. The system generates a random 10-character alphanumeric password and automatically emails it to the user's inbox alongside their login link.

Modifying Access

Click the icon to open a user's profile. You can change their Department, Contact Number, or promote them to a new Role. You can also manually overwrite their password here if they get locked out.

Suspending Accounts

If an employee resigns, click the toggle button. This changes their status to Suspended and immediately revokes their login access without deleting their historical audit trails.

11. Item Master

This module is used to define and maintain the Core Master Data that drives the entire Warehouse Management System.

A. Item Masterlist Management

The Item Masterlist stores all physical items recognized by the system. If an item is not registered here, it cannot be received, transferred, or sold.

Single Item Registration

Click Register New Item. Define the SKU, Description, UOM, Category, and Minimum Stock Level.

Bulk Excel Import

Click Import Excel. Download the system template, fill it with your items, and re-upload. The system automatically creates new items or updates existing ones.

B. Initial Opening Balance

When registering an item for the very first time, the form includes an Opening Stock Qty field. Filling this out automatically creates a system-generated Material Tag (Lot No: OB-YYMMDD) to establish your starting physical inventory without needing a Purchase Order.

12. Customer Master Data

Manage the directory of registered clients, their delivery addresses, and logistics routing information.

A. Registering a Client

Click Add Customer. The required fields include the Company Name, Contact Person, and full Delivery Address.

Client Code: You can assign a unique alphanumeric code to the client (e.g. CLI-001). This can be used later for barcode scanning and faster data entry when creating Sales Orders.

B. Logistics Routing

To ensure faster Outbound operations, you can pre-configure the client's routing logic:

  • Route Code / Zone: Define the client's geographical area (e.g., METRO MANILA, NORTH LUZON). This groups shipments together.
  • Preferred Transport: Set the default delivery method (Standard, Cargo Truck, Van, Motorcycle, or Client Pick-up).

C. Managing Existing Clients

You can modify a client's details by clicking the icon. To prevent them from making new orders, untick the Set as Active Client checkbox.

Warning: Deleting a client is a permanent action and may affect historical sales data. Only delete a client if they were added by mistake.

13. Supplier Master Data

Maintain the directory of all registered vendors and suppliers. This is crucial for creating Purchase Orders and managing inbound delivery expectations.

A. Registering a Supplier

Click Register Supplier. Ensure that the Vendor Code is unique, as this serves as the primary identifier in the system.

Logistics Status

Set the Average Lead Time (in days) during registration. The system uses this to estimate delivery dates and predict delayed shipments in the Logistics Calendar.

Active Status

If a supplier is no longer partnered with your company, untick the "Active Vendor" checkbox to prevent new Purchase Orders from being issued to them.

Managing Records

To edit details, click the icon. It is highly recommended to DEACTIVATE a supplier rather than deleting them , as deleting will affect historical PO logs.

14. Location Master (Warehouse Layout)

The Location Master defines the physical layout of your warehouses. This is where you configure zones, racks, and bins to optimize inventory placement.

A. Registering Storage Slots

Click Add New Slot to define a new location. You must input the following critical details:

  • Rack / Bin Code: The unique alphanumeric barcode (e.g., RACK-A12).
  • Warehouse & Zone: The building and specific area (e.g., MAIN WAREHOUSE, ZONE A).
  • Max Capacity: The total number of items (in PCS) that can physically fit in this location.

B. Capacity Monitoring

The system automatically tracks the live utilization of each rack based on the assigned Max Capacity.

  • Green = Available Space
  • Yellow = Almost Full (>80%)
  • Red = Rack is Full (100%)

C. Managing Locations

You can safely edit the descriptions or capacity of a rack at any time. However, you cannot delete a location if there are still physical items stored inside it. You must transfer all stocks out of the rack before deletion is allowed.

15. Notification Routing

This module allows administrators to fine-tune who receives what alerts. Instead of spamming everyone, you can individually assign system events (like PO Approvals or Low Stock warnings) to specific users via Email or In-App Web Alerts.

A. Modifying User Alerts

In the Notification Routing masterlist, search for a specific employee and click Manage Alerts. This will open the Configuration Matrix.

B. The Configuration Matrix

The modal displays a list of all system events. For each event, you can tick the checkboxes to route notifications to the user via:

Email Alerts

Sends a direct message to their registered email address. Best for critical approvals or external notifications.

Web App Alerts

Triggers the red notification bell inside the WMS dashboard while they are logged in.

* Remember to click Apply Matrix to save the new routing rules.

16. Role-Based Access Control (ACL)

Administrators use this module to precisely define which parts of the system an employee can see and interact with. This prevents unauthorized personnel from accessing sensitive data or functions.

A. Modifying Module Permissions

In the ACL masterlist, click the settings icon next to a user. This opens the Configuration Modal where you can tick or untick specific modules.

Super Admin Status

When Super Admin Access is granted, the user receives unrestricted permission to ALL current and future modules. All modular checkboxes will be locked and enabled by default.

Modular Granularity

For regular staff, you can pick specific modules (e.g., Receiving and Inquiry only). They will only see these options in their main navigation menu; all other parts of the system will be invisible to them.

Security Note: Once permissions are updated, the target user will receive an instant In-App Notification informing them of the change. Changes take effect immediately upon their next page refresh.

17. System Audit Logs (Security Tracking)

The System Audit Log is a read-only historical record of every critical action performed within the WMS. It ensures complete transparency and accountability for all registered users.

A. Security Filtering & Search

Located at System Modules > Audit Logs. Use the filter bar to isolate specific events:

  • Action Filter: View only specific triggers like CREATE, UPDATE, DELETE, or LOGIN.
  • Quick Search: Search by Username to track an employee's daily activity, or by Module (e.g., "Purchase Order") to see its history.

B. Understanding the Activity Log

Each log entry provides four critical pieces of data:

1. Timestamp & User

The exact date and time (down to the second) and the account name responsible for the action.

2. Action & Module

Categorizes the event (e.g., UPDATE) and identifies which part of the ERP was affected.

3. Activity Description

A detailed narrative of what exactly was changed (e.g., "Updated unit price for item RM-101").

4. IP Address Tracking

Logs the unique network address of the device used. This is critical for detecting unauthorized remote access.

Security Protocol: Audit logs cannot be edited or deleted by any user, including Super Admins. This ensures a "Single Version of Truth" for company audits and investigations.

20. Cycle Counting (Inventory Reconciliation)

Cycle Counting is an internal auditing procedure where a small subset of inventory in a specific location is counted on a specific day. This ensures continuous inventory accuracy without doing a full warehouse shutdown.

A. Execution Workflow

Step Action / Result
Select Location Choose the Active Location (Rack/Bin) where you are physically performing the count.
Scan SKU Scan the Item barcode. The system performs an AJAX Lookup to retrieve the "System Quantity" (what the computer thinks you have).
Input Actual Type the actual number of pieces you found in the bin and press [Enter].
Review Variance The Audit Grid will automatically calculate the variance (SHORT or OVER).

Variance Detection

If the actual count is different from the system count, the system flags it in the audit grid. Clicking Post Audit will automatically create a CORR (Correction) log to adjust the inventory level to match your physical count.

Ghost Stock Auto-Entry

If you scan an item in a bin where the system says it should not exist (System Qty = 0), the WMS will automatically register it as a **New Discovery**. A new Material Tag will be generated (Pre-fixed with AUDIT-) to bring that stock into the system.

Finalizing the Audit

Once you have scanned all items in the bin, click Post Audit. This will:

  • Update physical quantities in the Material Tags table.
  • Write entries into the Stock Movement History for audit tracking.
  • Generate a System Audit Log entry under the 'Cycle Count' module.

21. Fleet Management & Logistics Control

This module tracks the physical assets used for delivery, the assigned crew, and the operational expenses of each dispatch trip.

A. Fleet Registration & Compliance

Register company vehicles by defining the Plate Number, Vehicle Type, and Maximum Capacity (KG).

Coding Logic

Assign the Coding Day. The system will automatically hide vehicles from the dispatch list on their restricted days to prevent violations.

Maintenance (PMS/LTO)

Input LTO Expiry and Next PMS dates. The system flags vehicles in red if they are overdue for registration or maintenance.

B. Crew Assignment

Click Edit Crew on the masterlist to assign a registered Driver and an Assistant (Pahinante) to a specific vehicle. A driver must be registered in the Driver Masterlist first.

C. Live Trip Monitor (Google Maps)

When a vehicle is In Transit, click the Trip Info button to open the Command Center view:

  • View the Real-time Map Location of the delivery address.
  • See the Manifest (Items on Board) for that specific trip.
  • Access Get Route Directions to assist the driver via navigation apps.

D. Logistics Expense Logging

Upon returning from a trip, the dispatcher must log Fuel Costs and Toll Fees. These expenses are permanently linked to the Main PO Batch to provide management with a detailed Cost-Per-Delivery analysis in the Analytics module.

22. Reverse Logistics (RMA)

The Return Merchandise Authorization (RMA) module handles items coming back to the warehouse (Inbound Customer Returns) or being sent back to suppliers (Outbound Vendor Returns).

Phase 1: RMA Ticket Creation

Go to RMA Dashboard > Create Ticket. Select the Return Type (Customer or Supplier) and enter the Reference Order No.

Pro-Tip: Clicking the search icon after entering the Reference No. will auto-fetch all items bought in that order, allowing you to simply check the items being returned.

Phase 2: Quarantine Receipt

When the items physically arrive at the warehouse, the Receiver must click Receive on the RMA Dashboard.

  • Verify the physical quantity against the expected quantity.
  • The system automatically creates a new Material Tag (pre-fixed with RMA-).
  • All items are initially routed to the QUARANTINE ZONE to prevent them from being accidentally sold before inspection.

Phase 3: QC Disposition & Resolution

Quality Control (QC) staff must evaluate each item and apply two types of decisions:

Physical Disposition
  • Restock: Move item back to a saleable rack.
  • Scrap: Dispose of damaged inventory (Qty = 0).
  • RTV: Stage for return to the supplier.
Financial Resolution
  • Refund: Mark for accounting credit.
  • Replace: Triggers the Auto-Replacement Logic.
  • Write-Off: Mark as total loss.

Smart Auto-Replacement Synergy

When "Replace" is selected as a resolution, the WMS AI automatically searches for the nearest incoming Supplier PO for that SKU. It creates an instant Cross-Dock Link so that the replacement item is intercepted at the dock and sent straight to the customer, bypassing regular storage to save time.

3. Logistics Tracker

24. Global Scan & Direct Receiving

The Global Scanner is a fast-action tool accessible from anywhere in the system. It allows warehouse staff to rapidly lookup items or instantly receive/put-away inventory without navigating through multiple menus.

A. The Universal Scanner

Click the Scan Item icon from the floating Capsule Menu. This opens the Item Lookup Scanner overlay.

Hardware Scanner: Simply point your USB/Bluetooth barcode gun and scan. The system automatically reads the input and processes it instantly.
Web Camera Support: In forms requiring barcodes, clicking the camera icon will open your laptop or tablet's webcam to scan QR codes and barcodes directly.

B. Direct Put-Away

When receiving items on the fly (Direct Receipt), use the Receiving & Put-Away scanner module:

  1. Scan the Target Location barcode (the physical rack or bin).
  2. Scan the Item SKU.
  3. Input the quantity and press Register. The system instantly updates live stock levels.

C. Cross-Dock Auto-Interception (AI Logic)

This is the system's most powerful routing feature. When you scan an item for Put-Away, the WMS AI checks in the background if there is a pending, urgent Customer Order waiting for that specific item.

Interception: If a match is found, the system blocks normal Put-Away and triggers a red CROSS-DOCK ALERT on your screen.

Customer & Fleet Notification: It automatically sends an "Arrival Notice" email to the customer and triggers an In-App web alert to your Fleet Logistics team for immediate pickup.

Stock Splitting: If the received quantity is larger than the waiting order, the AI automatically splits the batch—allocating the exact amount to the Dispatch Staging Area and routing the excess back to your scanned rack.

Special Print: Instead of a standard bin tag, the system redirects to print a special Cross-Dock Label to stick on the box.

25. Lot Traceability Explorer

The Traceability Explorer is an advanced auditing tool that acts as a search engine for your inventory's genealogy. It tracks the complete lifecycle of a physical box or batch from the moment it enters the warehouse until it is dispatched or consumed.

A. Smart Lookup System

The search bar is equipped with dual-lookup capabilities. You can search using two methods:

Exact Lot Number: Enter a specific Material Tag ID (e.g., L-2605-1234) to instantly pull up its detailed tracking page.
Item Code Search: Enter a general SKU. The system will display a table of all available, past, and present lots associated with that item so you can choose which one to investigate.

B. Current Status & Origin

Once a lot is selected, the left panel displays its immediate profile:

  • Remaining Stock & QC: See exactly how many pieces are left in this specific box and its inspection status.
  • Last Known Location: The exact physical rack or zone where this box is currently stored.
  • Origin Source: Identifies the exact Supplier and Main Purchase Order (Batch ID) from which this item was acquired.

C. The Lifecycle Timeline

The right panel displays a chronological, uneditable history of all transactions involving this lot:

  • Registration (REG): When the item was first received and tagged.
  • Movement (MOVE): Transfers from one rack to another.
  • Deduction (OUT): Every time stock was picked for an order or request.

Note: Each timeline entry includes the exact timestamp, the user responsible, and the running balance of the stock.

26. UOM & Packaging Calculator

This standalone utility tool is designed for warehouse checkers and dispatchers to quickly convert large quantities of loose items into their corresponding commercial packaging units (Pallets and Boxes).

A. Input Parameters

  • • Item Lookup: Optional search to pull standard packaging data from the Item Master.
  • • Total Pieces: Enter the grand total count of loose items to be packed.
  • • Custom Multipliers: Manually adjust how many PCS fit in a BOX, and how many BOXES fit on a PALLET.

Calculation Export

27. CBM & Fleet Load Calculator

The CBM Calculator is a logistics optimization tool used to simulate truck loading. It ensures that outgoing shipments do not exceed the vehicle's weight capacity (KG) and volume capacity (Cubic Meters).

1. Vehicle Selection

Select a vehicle from the Company Fleet presets to auto-load its maximum KG and CBM capacity. You may also manually override these values for third-party couriers.

2. Auto-Load Order (API)

Instead of manual entry, type the Order Number / Main P.O. and click "Fetch Order". The system will automatically retrieve all items in that order and compute their total weight and volume based on the Item Master data.

Live Load Visualizer

Weight Progress: Monitors the total KG. Turns RED if the payload exceeds the vehicle's suspension limit.

Volume Progress (CBM): Monitors the total space occupied. Turns RED if the items will not physically fit inside the truck body.

Over-Capacity Warning: An animated alert triggers when 100% load is reached.

Manual Dimension Calculator

For non-standard items, enter the Length, Width, and Height (in cm). The system uses the formula:
(L × W × H) / 1,000,000 = CBM
